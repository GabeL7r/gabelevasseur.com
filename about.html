Professional Life

University of Maine Orono

Bachelor of Science, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Minor in Mathematics

I completed a five-year program in three years. Looking back, I regret not trying to triple major in three years.


Portsmouth Naval Shipyard

Nuclear Shift Test Engineer

I completed a two-year training program to become a certified Nuclear Shift Test Engineer (STE). Afterward, I directed a team of Navy and Shipyard personal to conduct maintenance and testing on Nuclear systems. The most exciting part was directing the startup and increase to 100% power of a nuclear reactor.


However, the work/life balance and working conditions of an STE are unpleasant, so I became a trainer.


I trained people to do the job I didn't enjoy doing.


I also got to ride in a submarine from England to the US.


Nuclear Regulatory Commission

Reactor Operations Engineer

I went through a six-month training program to qualify as a Reactor Operations Engineer. Afterword I read a lot of documents, went to a lot of meetings and realized I was not meant for government work.


One day while at work, I read that Actuary was the most desirable job. The only requirement: be good at Math. I studied for the Society of Actuary exams and later enrolled in graduate school.


A different day while at work, I came up with an idea for a fitness application: personalized and unique daily workouts based on the user's goals. "How hard could it be to build an application? I took a few programming classes in college," I thought. Famous last words before I started the journey to create it.

Software Engineer

Things were different here, to say the least. First of all, I was on a team with more than three people. I learned. I adapted. I thrived.

I organized a weekly developer training for Engineers to learn from each other. I worked with Angular, React, Java, Jenkins, AWS, CodeceptJS, Wordpress, PHP and more.
